About Me

Hi! My name is Kiswa Kabir. I'm a fourth-year student at Northeastern University's Khoury College of Computer Sciences, studying Computer Science and Business Administration with a concentration in Management. I'm expected to graduate in April 2027.

Through Northeastern's co-op program, I recently completed a Backend Software Engineering co-op at Priceline, where I worked on their Mobility (Rental Cars) division. I built a Kafka-to-BigQuery data pipeline that processed over 1.2 million events per day, and developed an Airport Counter Data Quality system that flagged mapping discrepancies across 100+ locations. Seeing those systems run in production was one of the most rewarding experiences I've had as an engineer so far.

Outside of co-op, I work as a Software Developer at the Social Equity Action Lab (SEAL) at Northeastern, helping build and maintain the lab's website. I'm also part of Northeastern Electric Racing (NER) and involved in the NUWIT Mentorship Program, which pairs upperclassmen with first-year women in tech.

I care a lot about using technology for social good — that thread runs through most of the work I do, from SEAL to the kinds of projects I choose to build on my own time. When I'm not coding, I'm usually cooking something new, reading, taking photos around Boston, or thinking about sustainability. I love the energy of this city and feel lucky to be studying and working here. I'm always looking for opportunities to build things that actually matter, and I'm excited about where this field is headed.
