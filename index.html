About Me

Hi, I'm Kiswa Khan! I'm currently a third-year at Northeastern University, studying Computer Science and Business Administration with a concentration in Management. I spend most of my time between Connecticut and Boston, but I've moved a lot with my family growing up — an experience that has taught me to be adaptable and open-minded, qualities that really shape how I approach learning and problem-solving in tech.

Over the past few years, I've built a strong foundation in software development through coursework, co-ops, and personal projects. Most recently, I completed my first co-op with a travel tech company, where I worked as a Backend Software Engineering intern on the Mobility and Rental Cars team. That experience let me apply everything I'd learned in school while pushing me to grow far beyond what any classroom could teach — from building data pipelines processing over a million daily events to improving internal tooling used by teams across 100+ airport locations.

I'm currently working as a Software Developer at the Social Equity Action Lab and at Northeastern Electric Racing, where I'm contributing to real projects that sit at the intersection of technology and impact. I'm also deeply interested in databases, data engineering, and the way thoughtful software can drive meaningful change — whether that's in urban data analysis, social equity, or consumer tech.

Outside of work and school, I'm passionate about nutrition and cooking, and I love to travel. I'm always looking for opportunities to continue growing as a developer, take on tangible projects, and collaborate with people doing work that actually matters. If that sounds like you or your team, I'd love to connect!
